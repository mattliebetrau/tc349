Good & Bad Design

Matt Liebetrau

The Good

Signal-to-Noise Ratio

A List Apart does a great job of presenting their content in a very direct manner with minimal distractions. The reader doesn't have to cut through visual clutter to get to where they want to be. This makes reading much easier and makes the experience pleasant and painless.

A List Apart

Chunking

Sitepoint's navigation shows a great example of chunking content into rational sections to make things easier to digest for users. The sections of the website are categorized by areas of interest that are common for web developers and designers which makes it easy for the creators to sort things and easy for the users to quickly find what they're looking for.

Sitepoint

Hick's Law

Virgin America found a great method of guiding the user through the flight booking process by offering a small amount of choices at once. By giving users less choices, they are less likely to become overwhelmed and will take less time booking their flight.

Virgin America

Affordance

ESPN FC uses small arrow icons in their navigation to let the user know there are more options available if they click on the navigation item. The downward pointing arrow is a common design convention and is widely recognized to mean there is more content to be revealed.

ESPN FC

Modularity

Mashable uses a very modular design structure. Each piece of content on their home page is in its own little self contained box which makes everything stand out on its own.

Mashable

The Bad

Signal-to-Noise Ratio

Yahoo (Japan) is a great example of how not to structure a large amount of content. The page is so busy that the user doesn't know where to start when looking around the website which creates a lot of confusion and frustration.

Yahoo (Japan)

Chunking

Reddit, while having content split up into sections via subreddits, doesn't visually chunk content on its home page very well. To a non-Redditor, the website can look like an ugly, intimidating heap of links.

Reddit

Hick's Law

United Airlines floods the user with options to search for flights which can be overwhelming when you only know the departure and arrival locations. Having too many options too early in the booking process can deter a user from using a service and cause them to leave the website and look elsewhere.

United Airlines

Affordance

Jet's Pizza has three callouts on their main page that appear to be clickable, since they are advertising things the restaurant offers. However, when you click on them nothing happens. This can frustrate the customer and cause Jet's to easily lose out on a sale.

Jet's Pizza

Modularity

Rhode Island College's campus store is a bad example of modular design. The website looks like it was just added to as the developers went along with no clear plan as to how they wanted to structure their content.

RIC Campus Store
